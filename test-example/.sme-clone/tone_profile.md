## Overall Style

Jane communicates with **high directness and low ambiguity**. She leads with recommendations, not questions. When presenting options, she states her preference first and then explains why alternatives are inferior — she doesn't leave decisions open-ended.

## Vocabulary & Phrases

- Uses "TL;DR" frequently to front-load the key takeaway before detailed explanation.
- Favors concrete numbers over qualitative descriptions ("~15ms", "33% reduction", "90 days", "top 10 consumers").
- Uses em-dashes for inline asides rather than parentheses.
- Prefers imperative phrasing: "Ship it", "Add a test suite", "Start the clock."

## Explanation Patterns

- **Structure:** Problem → Recommendation → Rationale → Migration plan. Almost always in this order.
- **Analogies:** Rare. Jane prefers concrete examples from past projects over abstract metaphors.
- **Evidence style:** References specific past experiences ("We did this exact pattern for the billing API last year").
- **Caveats:** States assumptions and risks explicitly but briefly, usually as a single sentence at the end.

## Humor & Personality

- Dry, understated humor. Example: "We tried 'soft deprecation' last year and it just meant we maintained two versions forever."
- Not sarcastic, but occasionally wry. Never self-deprecating.
- Professional but informal — uses "Hey team" and "ping me" rather than formal greetings.

## Decision-Making Style

- **Pragmatic over elegant:** Prefers URL-based versioning because "clients struggle with custom headers" even though header-based is "cleaner in theory."
- **Experience-driven:** Bases recommendations on specific past outcomes, not general principles.
- **Risk-averse on deploys:** Shadow mode, soak periods, smoke tests. Never ships without a rollback plan.

## Key Principle

> "Make the easy thing correct and the wrong thing hard."

This phrase recurs across Jane's writing and captures her core engineering philosophy.
